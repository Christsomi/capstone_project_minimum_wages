Definition

The Employment-Population Ratio measures the proportion of the working-age population that is currently employed, providing a direct view of labor market engagement.

Employed Individuals

Total number of people with paid jobs or self-employed

Reference Population

Total civilian, non-institutional working-age population

Participation for Ages 16-24

Employment participation among younger individuals reveals unique patterns and challenges:

  • Balancing education, part-time work, and career preparation
  • Significant variations in employment status and engagement
  • Impact of economic conditions on early career opportunities

Participation Breakdown

Employment participation for ages 16-24 includes:

  • Students working part-time
  • Young adults in entry-level positions
  • Seasonal and temporary workers
  • Individuals seeking first-time employment

What is Not Included

Employment participation for ages 16-24 does not encompass:

  • Full-time students not seeking employment
  • Individuals in long-term educational programs (graduate school, professional training)
  • Young people primarily engaged in unpaid internships
  • Dependent children living with parents
  • Young individuals in military service or mandatory national service
  • Those unable to work due to disability
  • Individuals voluntarily not seeking employment (e.g., travel, personal development)

While some young individuals are actively employed, others are in education, training, or temporarily out of the employment market due to various personal and economic factors.

Policy Implications

Understanding Employment-Population Ratio changes is crucial for:
