Definition

Total separations measure the number of employees leaving employment during a specific time period. This includes voluntary resignations, involuntary terminations, and retirements.

Voluntary Resignations

Employees choosing to leave their jobs for personal or professional reasons.

Involuntary Terminations

Dismissals initiated by employers due to performance or other reasons.

Retirements

Planned exits from the workforce due to reaching retirement age or other factors.

ðŸ”¢

Formula

Total Separations = Voluntary Resignations + Involuntary Terminations + Retirements

Calculated by:

  • Summing all employee exits during the time period
  • Including all forms of separation
  • Excluding internal transfers or promotions

Implications for Youth

Total separations data has critical implications for the younger workforce:

  • Higher Turnover Insight: Highlighting industries where young workers face higher turnover.
  • Education Alignment: Informing educational programs to prepare youth for stable careers.
  • Retention Strategies: Encouraging youth-specific job retention policies, such as mentorship programs.
  • Skill Gap Opportunities: Identifying sectors with potential skill gaps that young professionals can fill.
