Youth Labor Market Analysis Variables

Key indicators for analyzing minimum wage impacts on youth employment (Ages 16-24)

Youth Labor Market Statistics (LEU)

Core indicators of youth labor market performance and workforce participation (Ages 16-24)

Labor Force Participation Rate

Percentage of population aged 16-24 that is either employed or actively seeking employment.

Research Relevance

Crucial indicator of how minimum wage changes affect young people's decisions to enter the labor market.

Employment/Population Ratio

Proportion of the youth population (16-24) that is currently employed.

Research Relevance

Direct measure of youth employment outcomes and minimum wage effects.

Unemployment Rate

Percentage of youth labor force (16-24) that is unemployed but actively seeking employment.

Research Relevance

Critical measure for assessing disemployment effects of minimum wage policies.

Youth Employment Dynamics (ED)

Detailed metrics on youth job flows and workforce stability (Ages 16-24)

Beginning of Quarter Employment: Counts

Number of young workers (16-24) employed at the start of each quarter.

Research Relevance

Provides baseline employment levels for tracking minimum wage effects.

Hires All: Counts

Total number of new hires for young workers (16-24) in a given period.

Research Relevance

Measures job market entry and hiring dynamics for youth.

Separations: Counts

Total number of job separations for young workers (16-24).

Research Relevance

Captures job market exit and workforce mobility for youth.

Turnover (Stable)

Measure of job stability for young workers (16-24).

Research Relevance

Evaluates workforce retention and job market stability.

Firm Job Gains: Counts (Job Creation)

Number of jobs created by firms for young workers (16-24).

Research Relevance

Measures job market expansion and economic growth potential.

Firm Job Loss: Counts (Job Destruction)

Number of jobs lost by firms for young workers (16-24).

Research Relevance

Captures job market contraction and economic challenges.

Firm Job Change: Net Change

Net change in jobs for young workers (16-24) across firms.

Research Relevance

Provides overall picture of job market dynamics and growth.

Full Quarter Employment (Stable): Average Monthly Earnings

Average monthly earnings for young workers (16-24) employed full quarter.

Research Relevance

Measures income stability and economic impact of employment policies.

Educational Indicators (EI)

Metrics tracking youth educational participation (Ages 16-24)

High School Dropout Rates

State-level dropout rates for ages 16-19.

Research Relevance

Examines education-employment tradeoffs due to wage changes.

School Enrollment Status

High school and college enrollment rates (16-24).

Research Relevance

Analyzes education decisions relative to minimum wage levels.

Educational Attainment

Completion rates for high school and college (16-24).

Research Relevance

Measures long-term educational impacts of wage policies.
